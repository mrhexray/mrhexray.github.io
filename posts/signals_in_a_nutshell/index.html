Signals in a nutshell

8 Jun 2026

Signals

In simple terms, signal is a notification sent to a process either by the kernel or another process. It notifies the receiving process about some event which needs urgent attention. When a process gets a signal, it pauses its current work to execute a designated “signal handler” before returning to its normal flow

Signals vs Interrupt

FeatureSignalInterrupt
DescriptionSignal is a software mechanism of communicating about events between processes or OS kerenlInterrupt is a hardware mechanism to communicate between device (peripherals) and the CPU.
OriginSoftware (kernel or process)Hardware (Peripherals or CPU itself)
TargetA specific running processCPU or kernel
PurposeTo notify process of an eventTo get immediate attention of CPU
HandlingHandled in user space by the receiving processHandled kernel space by a driver or OS kernel

Analogy

  • Signal: A colleague walking up to your desk and handing you a urgent message to read. You stop your current work, read the message, take action, and then go back to what you were doing.
  • Interrupt: A fire alarm suddenly going off in a building. The alarm (hardware) forces you to stop what you are doing (CPU task) and evacuate immediately.
